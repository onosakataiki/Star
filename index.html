Prediction Challenge 🧠
Let's see if you can apply the Inverse Square Law (1/d²)!

Question 1

If you move a spaceship 2x further away from a star, what happens to the star's brightness?
Correct!
Distance is 2. The light spreads over 2² = 4 squares. So intensity is 1/4.

Question 2

What if you move 3x further away? How bright is it now?
Exactly!
Distance is 3. Area = 3² = 9. Intensity drops to 1/9.

Final Challenge

Star A is 10 pc away. Star B is 30 pc away. Both have the exact same true brightness. Which appears brighter to us, and by how much?
Brilliant!
Star B is 3 times further away than Star A. Because of the Inverse Square Law (3² = 9), Star A appears 9 times brighter than B!
